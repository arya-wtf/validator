## Per-idea analysis

### Idea #1: Waitlist Launch Kit

**Hypothesis:** Early-stage founders will pay for a fast, structured validation package if it removes the blank-page work and gives them a launch-ready test in under a week.

**Assumed buyer:** Indie hacker, agency owner turning one internal pain into a product, or solo founder with audience-light distribution

**Assumed pain:** They have an idea and maybe a channel, but their workaround is posting vague interest checks or overbuilding a landing page from scratch; cost is 1-3 weeks lost plus false signal from sloppy validation.

**Assumed pricing:** $149 template-only, $500-$900 productized service, because this buyer often has some budget but not enough for full strategy consulting; service tier matches current operator-led delivery motion.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First 10 buyers come from existing client/repeat-client network and adjacent operators like Gunnar, TJ, Son, plus outbound on Upwork/Contra to founders asking for landing page, launch, or messaging help.

**Strongest assumption:** Founders care enough about pre-build validation to pay for a lightweight package instead of DIYing with ChatGPT and Notion.

**Weakest assumption:** A validation metrics dashboard is valuable at this stage; most buyers may only want copy plus launch checklist, not another dashboard.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Manual Service - close 3 paid pilots at $300+ each within 14 days from warm network and outbound, with delivery using docs/templates before building software.

**Kill signals:**
- Warm contacts like TJ, Gunnar, or Son say the problem is real but would not pay because ChatGPT already covers it
- More than 70% of sales calls drift into custom go-to-market consulting instead of a repeatable package
- Buyers only want landing page copy and ignore the waitlist questions, email sequence, and metrics layer

**Founder-fit notes:** Strong fit because Dewi already does messaging, copywriting, outbound, onboarding, and proposal shaping. Low design dependence if delivered as copy packs plus simple no-code page structure. Weak spot is if buyers expect channel strategy depth or paid acquisition planning beyond current scope.


## Ranking

**Idea #1 — PROCEED FIRST**
This is the closest to current operator muscle: copy, packaging, scoping, and fast client-facing delivery. Distribution is also the cleanest because warm contacts already ask for tool/process guidance and budget-constrained scope cuts in favor of a smaller fixed offer. The software angle should wait; sell it manually first.


## What to do next

- **This week:** Pitch a fixed-scope paid pilot to 10 warm contacts and founder-adjacent leads: 'I’ll turn your idea into a 7-day validation kit with page copy, waitlist flow, launch posts, and success criteria.'
- **This month:** Standardize delivery from 3 paid pilots into one repeatable SOP, then strip out the lowest-value component and see if buyers still convert at the same price.
- **Open question:** Is the real buyer a founder validating a new product, or an agency/operator packaging internal ideas into spinout products?

## Constraints flagged

- This idea is service-first, not product-first; real risk is building a thin wrapper around work clients expect to be customized
- Design is a constraint, so the offer must avoid promising visual assets beyond reusable templates or wireframe-level page structure
- If the target buyer needs deep technical scoping before launch, Dewi may still depend on Arya and lose speed
- Buyer budgets are likely small, so pricing and scope discipline matter more than feature breadth
