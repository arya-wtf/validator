## Per-idea analysis

### Idea #1: From Freelancer to Studio Kit

**Hypothesis:** Solo freelance designers who are hitting delivery bottlenecks will pay for a practical ops kit that helps them add contractors, standardize delivery, and package services without needing full-time hires.

**Assumed buyer:** Independent brand/web designer earning roughly $2k-8k/month, juggling multiple clients, and considering subcontractors for the first time

**Assumed pain:** Trigger event: they get more inquiries or bigger retainers than they can handle alone; workaround: patchwork Notion docs, ad hoc freelancer help, and custom pricing per client; cost: delayed projects, inconsistent margins, and fear of saying yes to more work

**Assumed pricing:** $79-149 one-time template kit, or $249 with 1 live workshop/review, because this buyer is budget-sensitive and already negotiates scope hard

**Distribution hypothesis:** First 10 buyers come from current operator network: Gunnar, TJ, Jen, Son, Karis can each point to freelance designers in their circles; secondary channel is direct outbound on Dribbble/Contra to designers visibly working solo with case-study-heavy profiles

**Strongest assumption:** Design freelancers will buy implementation structure from a non-designer operator if the kit is clearly operational rather than creative

**Weakest assumption:** The buyer feels this pain acutely enough to pay now instead of continuing with free templates and messy improvisation

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Pre-order - sell 5 copies at $79+ from a landing page plus manual onboarding call within 14 days

**Kill signals:**
- Less than 3 of 15 target designers agree to a call after direct outreach
- Calls reveal they want lead generation or pricing advice only, not delivery ops
- Pre-order page gets interest but zero payment after 2 rounds of offer revision

**Founder-fit notes:** Good fit on ops, onboarding, proposals, workflow design, and client management. Weak fit on audience trust because the product targets designers and you explicitly do not do design. You can still win if positioning is 'operator kit for design freelancers' rather than 'design business coaching'.
