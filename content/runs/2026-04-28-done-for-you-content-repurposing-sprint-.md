## Per-idea analysis

### Idea #1: Done-for-you Content Repurposing Sprint

**Hypothesis:** A small UI/UX agency founder will pay for a fixed-scope sprint that converts one shipped project into 10-20 ready-to-post assets faster than they will buy a DIY kit.

**Assumed buyer:** Founder of a 2-10 person design or productized agency posting inconsistently on LinkedIn, X, Instagram, and Dribbble

**Assumed pain:** After a project ships, the team says they should publish it, but the workaround is letting assets sit in Figma/Dribbble while the founder occasionally posts ad hoc; cost is weak top-of-funnel visibility and constant reliance on referrals or outbound.

**Assumed pricing:** $250-$600 one-time sprint for 1 project repurposed into a content pack; low enough for budget-sensitive agencies, high enough to validate willingness to pay before building software or templates.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First 10 buyers come from existing client network and warm operators like TJ, Gunnar, Jen, Son, plus outreach on Dribbble/Contra to agencies already posting finished work but inconsistently.

**Strongest assumption:** Design agencies value speed and operator execution enough to pay for finished content assets instead of figuring it out themselves.

**Weakest assumption:** They may not care about multi-channel content enough to pay cash unless lead gen pain is urgent right now.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Manual Service - sell 3 paid sprints at $250+ within 14 days from warm outreach and direct operator conversations.

**Kill signals:**
- Warm contacts say the problem is real but still won't pay even for a low-ticket fixed sprint.
- Buyers only want custom strategy calls instead of a repeatable productized deliverable.
- Delivery requires design/video work beyond templates and text repurposing to feel valuable.

**Founder-fit notes:** Strong fit because Dewi can execute end-to-end in text workflows, onboarding, and project management without new technical build. Also matches existing buyer access and avoids long sales cycles.

### Idea #2: Content Repurposing Kit for Design Agencies

**Hypothesis:** Freelance designers and small design agencies will buy a Notion template plus prompt pack if it helps them turn one project into weeks of content without needing a marketer.

**Assumed buyer:** Solo freelance designer or 2-5 person design agency owner with some audience ambition but limited budget

**Assumed pain:** They know each project could become LinkedIn posts, X threads, captions, and video scripts, but the workaround is staring at a blank page or posting only the final shot; cost is low consistency and wasted portfolio material.

**Assumed pricing:** $29-$79 one-time template pack; cheap enough for impulse purchase, but only works if distribution is strong because ticket size is small.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First 10 buyers come from personal network, Dribbble/Contra outreach, X posts documenting the framework, and direct messages to designers who recently posted case studies or Dribbble shots.

**Strongest assumption:** A narrow design-agency angle makes the kit feel specific enough to convert better than generic content templates.

**Weakest assumption:** This market is crowded with cheap templates, and the buyer may prefer free prompts from ChatGPT over paying for a static pack.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Pre-order - get 10 paid pre-orders at $39+ from direct outreach and content posts before building the full kit.

**Kill signals:**
- Prospects ask for examples but do not convert at a low pre-order price.
- Most interest comes from non-design operators, meaning the niche positioning is too narrow or wrong.
- Buyers say they need accountability or execution, not templates.

**Founder-fit notes:** Medium fit: Dewi can build the prompts, hooks, and calendar easily, but distribution is the hard part and low-ticket products need volume. Also design-agency positioning conflicts slightly with Dewi's strongest current network, which is broader operator/marketing buyers, not pure designers.

### Idea #3: Client Content Reuse Audit + Calendar

**Hypothesis:** Existing and former Elux clients will pay for a lightweight audit that turns their shipped projects, blogs, and assets into a 30-day reuse plan because they already trust Dewi and regularly ask for practical marketing help.

**Assumed buyer:** Current or former Elux client decision-maker in marketing, ops, or founder seat

**Assumed pain:** They have existing content and completed work scattered across docs, landing pages, and shipped projects, but the workaround is random publishing and constant reinvention; cost is missed reuse and more marketing effort than their budget allows.

**Assumed pricing:** $150-$400 one-time audit plus calendar, with optional monthly add-on at $100-$300 for execution support; aligned with their budget-sensitive behavior and trust-based upsell motion.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First 10 buyers come from Slack conversations with Gunnar, Jen, Son, TJ, and prior clients where Dewi already has credibility and direct access to the person feeling the pain.

**Strongest assumption:** Warm trust plus visible pain around budget and tool/process decisions will convert faster than selling a cold product to designers.

**Weakest assumption:** Clients may see this as part of existing retainer expectations rather than a separate paid product.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** LOI - secure 5 explicit yeses from current/past clients and close 3 paid audits at $150+ within 10 days.

**Kill signals:**
- Clients like the idea but insist it should be bundled into current work for free.
- The output gets treated as consulting advice with no repeatable format.
- No buyer asks for follow-on execution or update cycles after seeing the audit.

**Founder-fit notes:** Best fit with current access, operator trust, and budget reality. It reuses Dewi's existing content and PM skills, needs no design, and generates evidence faster than a cold template product.

### Idea #4: Monthly Content Engine Membership

**Hypothesis:** Small design agencies will subscribe to a monthly system of calendars, prompts, hooks, and examples if it removes planning overhead and gives them a reason to keep posting.

**Assumed buyer:** Agency owner who wants ongoing posting structure but cannot justify a full content retainer

**Assumed pain:** They repeatedly fall off content after one or two weeks, so the workaround is buying scattered templates or relying on last-minute brainstorming; cost is inconsistent brand presence and low compounding audience growth.

**Assumed pricing:** $19-$49/month membership for monthly calendars, prompt drops, and examples; low enough to try, but retention depends on real usage.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First 10 buyers come from buyers of the initial kit or service upsells, plus audience posts showing monthly planning examples.

**Strongest assumption:** Template buyers want recurring structure badly enough to keep paying every month.

**Weakest assumption:** This is a weak standalone wedge because subscriptions for planning tools churn fast unless tied to a workflow people already use weekly.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Pre-order - get 5 annual commitments or 15 monthly commitments before building a member area.

**Kill signals:**
- Interested buyers prefer one-time packs over subscriptions.
- Usage drops after the first month because prompts are not embedded in their workflow.
- Support requests turn it into a service business with low-margin maintenance.

**Founder-fit notes:** Medium fit operationally, but weak fit strategically right now. Dewi can produce the materials, yet recurring template businesses are retention-heavy and need audience scale that is not established.


## Ranking

**Idea #3 — PROCEED FIRST**
This is the shortest path to evidence because buyer access already exists in Slack with people who trust Dewi and openly share pain. It also fits the budget-sensitive reality: a small paid audit is easier to close than a cold digital product and can later be packaged into the kit if patterns repeat.

**Idea #1 — PROCEED IF X FAILS**
If warm clients will not pay for an audit, sell execution instead of advice. Agencies often pay faster for a finished content pack than for a template because it saves operator time immediately.

**Idea #2 — PARK**
The kit is buildable, but distribution is the problem and low-ticket products hide weak demand. Validate willingness to pay through service and audit offers first, then distill the recurring pieces into a product.

**Idea #4 — KILL**
A membership adds retention burden before proving that buyers even want the underlying workflow. It is the furthest from cash and the easiest for customers to cancel after one month.


## What to do next

- **This week:** Pitch a paid 'Content Reuse Audit + 30-Day Calendar' to 8 warm contacts in Slack/email, using one concrete before/after sample built from an existing client asset.
- **This month:** Run 3 paid audits, document repeated deliverables and objections, then decide whether the reusable core is a done-for-you sprint or a DIY kit.
- **Open question:** Do warm buyers value planning artifacts enough to pay separately, or do they only pay when execution is included?

## Constraints flagged

- Dewi cannot rely on net-new design creation, so any offer must work from existing assets, screenshots, case studies, and templates.
- Cold low-ticket template sales require audience or strong outbound volume, which is less certain than selling into existing client relationships.
- Long discovery-heavy sales are a bad fit, so the offer must be fixed-scope, priced simply, and easy to approve asynchronously.
- Technical scoping is a weak area, so avoid offers that require channel analytics tooling, integrations, or complex automation up front.
