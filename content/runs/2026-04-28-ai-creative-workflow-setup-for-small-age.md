AI Prompt Workflow Setup for Creative Teams

I help small agencies and freelance teams build a reusable AI prompt system for landing pages, campaign visuals, and short-form video assets.

You’ll get:
1. A prompt library based on your actual services and client use cases
2. A reusable prompt structure for landing pages, visuals, and video assets
3. A workflow for turning existing assets or references into new variations
4. A simple documentation system your team can reuse
5. Optional monthly maintenance to update and improve the system

Best for:
Small agencies or freelance teams that already use ChatGPT, Midjourney, Runway, or other AI tools, but still rely on scattered prompts, old chats, and manual rewriting.

## Per-idea analysis

### Idea #1: AI Creative Workflow Setup for Small Agencies

**Hypothesis:** Small agencies and freelance teams already using ChatGPT or similar AI tools will pay for a productized setup service that turns scattered prompt usage into a reusable workflow system focused on content, landing page copy, campaign ideation, and creative briefs, especially if it is delivered as a fast implementation rather than software.

**Assumed buyer:** Small agency founder, delivery lead, or operations lead at a small agency or freelance team already using ChatGPT or other AI tools in delivery work

**Assumed pain:** Trigger event: the team starts using AI repeatedly for content, landing page copy, campaign ideation, and creative briefs; workaround: prompts are stored in personal chats, Notion docs, and old files, with each person improvising; cost: repeated manual rewriting, inconsistent outputs, slower turnaround, poor knowledge transfer, and no searchable internal system the team can reuse.

**Assumed pricing:** $500-$1,500 one-time setup plus optional $100-$300/month maintenance, positioned as a productized service because the buyer set is budget-sensitive and more likely to approve scoped implementation than software.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First customers come from warm network contacts and adjacent operators who already ask for tooling and process advice; secondary distribution is outbound to small agencies and freelance teams on Upwork, Contra, and Dribbble with an offer framed as an AI workflow audit plus reusable documentation setup.

**Strongest assumption:** Small agencies feel enough pain from scattered AI usage and repeated rewriting that they will pay for a reusable workflow system instead of continuing with ad hoc prompts.

**Weakest assumption:** Even with tighter scope around content, landing page copy, campaign ideation, and creative briefs, buyers may still treat this as nice-to-have process cleanup unless the offer is tied to a clear speed or quality gain they can feel quickly.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Manual Service - close 3 paid pilot setups at $500+ within 21 days using a fixed deliverable: workflow audit, prompt library, prompt structures for content, landing page copy, campaign ideation, and creative briefs, plus SOP and searchable documentation hub.

**Kill signals:**
- Warm buyers agree the problem exists but only want informal advice bundled into existing work
- Buyers do not see enough urgency to pay for workflow setup as a standalone offer
- Delivery repeatedly requires custom scoping support from Arya, preventing a clean productized offer

**Founder-fit notes:** Good fit on content systems, prompt structure, documentation, onboarding, and process cleanup. Scope is now clearer and better aligned with your strengths because it stays centered on text-heavy workflows: content, landing page copy, campaign ideation, and creative briefs.
