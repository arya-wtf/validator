## Ideas Input

Portfolio Intelligence System

AI-powered portfolio system yang membantu designer dan small agency menganalisis, memperbarui, dan membangun portfolio yang lebih strategic. Produk ini bukan hanya membantu membuat case study, tetapi juga memberikan audit portfolio, trend analysis, gap analysis, rekomendasi positioning, dummy project brief, dan case study generator.

Masalah utama yang diselesaikan adalah banyak designer punya portfolio yang outdated, tidak punya arah positioning yang jelas, dan tidak tahu project seperti apa yang perlu dibuat untuk menarik client atau recruiter yang mereka incar. Mereka sering hanya meng-upload project lama tanpa tahu apakah portfolio tersebut masih relevan dengan market saat ini.

User dapat meng-upload portfolio existing seperti Behance link, PDF, atau website portfolio. Sistem akan menganalisis komposisi portfolio, melihat gap berdasarkan trend dan demand market, lalu memberikan rekomendasi seperti niche yang lebih potensial, jenis project yang perlu ditambahkan, dan angle positioning yang lebih kuat.

Produk ini terdiri dari tiga fungsi utama:

1. Portfolio Audit & Trend Analysis
    Menganalisis portfolio existing, menemukan gap, memberi insight trend, dan merekomendasikan positioning.
2. Dummy Project Brief Generator
    Membuat brief project fiktif yang realistis dan trend-driven, supaya designer bisa membuat portfolio baru yang relevan tanpa harus menunggu real client project.
3. Case Study Generator
    Mengubah project real maupun dummy menjadi case study siap pakai dengan struktur Challenge, Solution, Process, Results, serta output untuk Behance, website portfolio, dan social media.

Target market: freelance designer, UI/UX designer, product designer, branding designer, web designer, dan small creative agency yang ingin portfolio mereka lebih relevan, strategic, dan menjual.

## Per-idea analysis

### Idea #1: Proposal Scoping Copilot

**Hypothesis:** Lean agencies and freelance operators will pay for a tool-plus-service workflow that turns raw client inquiries into a recommended scope, pricing range, proposal draft, and risk flags because unclear scoping is delaying deals and increasing dependency on a more senior founder.

**Assumed buyer:** Marketing lead, agency founder, or freelance operator handling inbound leads and proposal writing for small-to-mid budget client work

**Assumed pain:** Client requests arrive incomplete, budgets are constrained, and technical complexity is hard to assess alone; the workaround is asking a senior partner for help, over-scoping, or spending too long rewriting proposals; the cost is slower turnaround, weaker confidence, and lost revenue from stalled or mismatched deals.

**Assumed pricing:** $99-$299/month for productized workflow or $49-$149 per scoped proposal in a manual-first service because the pain is directly tied to revenue and time-to-close.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First buyers come from existing client/operator network and adjacent agency peers who already ask for tool guidance, proposal help, or scoping support; warm Slack conversations and direct outreach outperform broad cold channels.

**Strongest assumption:** Scoping pain is frequent, urgent, and monetizable because it sits directly on the path to revenue.

**Weakest assumption:** Enough of the analysis can be standardized without deep technical review on every project.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Concierge MVP - get 5 paid scoping engagements or 3 monthly pilot users within 14 days from 15-20 warm operator conversations using a structured intake form and proposal output.

**Kill signals:**
- Operators say they only trust internal founders or technical leads for scoping decisions
- Every engagement requires bespoke technical analysis that cannot be templatized
- Buyers like the output but still do not send proposals faster or close more work

**Founder-fit notes:** Highest-fit idea. It maps directly to Dewi's stated pain, current workflow, and growth target. It also reduces dependence on Arya in exactly the area Dewi wants help: project analysis and proposal generation. The offer should stay focused on scope framing, options, and risk flags, not deep engineering estimation.

### Idea #2: Past Project Proof Hub

**Hypothesis:** Small agencies and freelance operators will pay for a searchable proof-of-work database that helps them retrieve relevant past projects, examples, outcomes, and proposal snippets fast because scattered institutional memory slows selling and weakens trust with leads.

**Assumed buyer:** Small agency founder, marketing lead, or client-facing operator with multiple past projects, repeat clients, and no clean internal project library

**Assumed pain:** When a lead asks for relevant examples, prior outcomes, or similar project experience, the team searches Slack, Notion, invoices, decks, and memory; the workaround is sending generic examples or delaying the reply; the cost is slower sales cycles, weaker credibility, and underused past work.

**Assumed pricing:** $49-$199 one-time setup plus $19-$79/month for maintained access, or internal-service packaging at $300-$1,000 for database cleanup and structuring.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First buyers come from service operators with repeat clients and messy archives, especially warm peers who already know they have underused proof-of-work. This can also be sold internally at Elux as the first live implementation.

**Strongest assumption:** Operators feel enough friction retrieving proof that they will pay to organize and operationalize past work, not just keep everything in ad hoc docs.

**Weakest assumption:** The problem is urgent enough to buy now rather than postpone as an internal admin task.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Manual setup - build 3 paid proof hubs for operators or agencies within 21 days and measure whether they use it in at least 3 live sales/proposal situations each.

**Kill signals:**
- Buyers agree it is useful but keep deprioritizing setup
- Past project data is too incomplete to make the hub meaningfully searchable
- Users do not actually open the hub during active sales conversations

**Founder-fit notes:** Very strong fit with Dewi's desire for a previous-project database and her PM/onboarding strengths. This is also credibility-safe because it is operational organization and sales enablement, not design or technical delivery.

### Idea #3: Content Reuse Engine for Lean Teams

**Hypothesis:** Lean service teams will pay for a simple system that turns one source asset into multiple channel-ready outputs because they know content matters but cannot maintain a repeatable reuse workflow while serving clients.

**Assumed buyer:** Solo operator, freelance marketer, or small agency team already publishing occasionally on blogs, X, LinkedIn, or newsletters

**Assumed pain:** They create useful ideas and client learnings but publishing is inconsistent because repurposing takes manual effort and context-switching; the workaround is posting sporadically or rewriting from scratch; the cost is weak audience growth and lower leverage from existing content work.

**Assumed pricing:** $39-$99/month for templates/workflow/software-lite or $200-$500 setup for a customized content reuse system.

**Distribution hypothesis:** First buyers come from service operators already in Dewi's network and from existing clients who have asked for content support or process help. Demonstrating Dewi's own workflow can drive trust.

**Strongest assumption:** Operators already believe content has strategic value and want a process more than a full agency service.

**Weakest assumption:** The pain is important enough to pay for now versus remaining a nice-to-have behind client delivery priorities.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** Pilot - sell 5 customized content reuse setups or workflow subscriptions within 21 days and track whether users publish at least twice as often within one month.

**Kill signals:**
- Buyers compliment the system but do not change publishing behavior
- Most demand turns into custom content creation services rather than reusable workflow needs
- Prospects treat generic AI tools as good enough and resist paying for structure

**Founder-fit notes:** High execution fit because Dewi can do text-based content end-to-end and understands cross-channel reuse. Slightly less urgent than proposal scoping because it is one step removed from immediate revenue.

### Idea #4: Operator OS for Lean Agencies

**Hypothesis:** A bundled workspace combining proposal scoping, past-project retrieval, and content reuse can become valuable if lean service teams want one operating layer for revenue-critical workflows instead of scattered docs, prompts, and spreadsheets.

**Assumed buyer:** 2-10 person agencies and advanced solo operators managing sales, delivery proof, and content with minimal ops support

**Assumed pain:** Key workflows live across Slack, Notion, Google Drive, and memory; proposal analysis is inconsistent, proof is hard to retrieve, and content systems break under delivery pressure; the cost is slower sales, duplicated effort, and founder dependency.

**Assumed pricing:** $99-$299/month per team because value depends on replacing multiple manual workflows, not just a single use case.

**Distribution hypothesis:** Only viable after validating one narrow wedge first, then expanding from existing scoping or proof-hub customers into a broader internal operating system.

**Strongest assumption:** The same buyer owns all three pains and prefers an integrated workflow over best-of-breed point solutions.

**Weakest assumption:** A broad bundle will not dilute urgency or make onboarding too heavy for small teams.

**Suggested Gate 3 test:** LOI/beta test - secure 5 beta commitments from users of the scoping copilot or proof hub before building any integrated product layer.

**Kill signals:**
- Buyers only want one feature and reject the broader bundle
- Onboarding requires too much data cleanup for small teams
- Usage clusters around one module with no pull for the rest

**Founder-fit notes:** Strategically aligned with Dewi's long-term desire to launch a product, but should come after the narrower, high-fit workflows are validated. Best viewed as the future container, not the first product.
